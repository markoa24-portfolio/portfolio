Group High-Powered Rocket (GHPR)

G-class rocket designed, fabricated, and launched with a safe payload recovery system. 3D-printed test rocket → fiberglass final vehicle.

Launch video — add MP4 or YouTube embed when ready
Project Overview

This project aimed to design, fabricate, and launch a G-class high-powered rocket capable of carrying a payload (an egg) to apogee and recovering it safely with a parachute system. The build combined simulation, CAD design, composites manufacturing, and systems integration into a full launch-ready vehicle.

To build confidence, we first completed a 3D-Printed Rocket (3DPR) as a mini test project, which allowed us to gain experience with OpenRocket simulations, SolidWorks CAD modeling, and parachute integration before scaling up to the full fiberglass GHPR.

Tools & Software
  • OpenRocket – used for rocket design, stability margin analysis, and flight simulations (apogee, velocity).
  • SolidWorks – CAD modeling of nose cone, body tube, fins, bulkheads, and centering rings.
  • Makerspace Tools – 3D printing, laser cutting, composite layup equipment.
Development Phase – 3D Printed Rocket (3DPR)
  • Designed in OpenRocket to meet stability criteria (1.10–2.50 Cal).
  • Modeled in SolidWorks, printed at Olympic Makerspace.
  • Fabricated a parachute by hand (cut fabric, stitched with string, attached to shock cord).
  • Launched successfully, reaching high apogee and validating parachute recovery.

Key Outcome: Provided hands-on practice with simulations, CAD-to-fabrication workflow, and recovery systems—directly informing GHPR design.

Main Project – Group High-Powered Rocket (GHPR)

Design Phase

  • Motor Selection: Aerotech G80-10T, suitable for G-class flights.
  • Simulations: OpenRocket required stability: 1.25–2.50 Cal; predicted apogee ~3,000 ft.
  • Subsystem CAD Assignments:
  • Nose Cone (Haack Series for drag reduction).
  • Body Tube (2 in. fiberglass layup).
  • Bulkhead & Middle Tube (egg housing).
  • Fins & Centering Rings (birch wood, laser cut).

Manufacturing Phase

Fiberglass Body Tube

  • Performed fiberglass layup on mandrel with epoxy + release film.
  • Sanded and trimmed tube to size.

Nose Cone

  • 3D printed Haack-series profile for minimized drag.
  • Iterated multiple versions for proper fit with body tube.

Fins & Centering Rings

  • Designed in SolidWorks → exported as DXF → laser cut birch wood.
  • Epoxied into body tube through precision slits.

Launch & Results

  • Apogee: 2,576 ft (from altimeter reading).
  • Velocity: 491 mph at peak ascent.
  • Recovery: Parachute deployed successfully; rocket recovered intact.
  • Payload: Egg payload was recovered without damage (adjusted).
