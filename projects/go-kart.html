Custom Go-Kart

Build video — add MP4 or YouTube embed when ready

From-scratch single-seat kart built around a Lifan 212 cc electric-start with a custom push-to-start tied into the Lifan harness, a 30-series torque converter driving a live axle, and a 1×1×.120″ A513 square-tube chassis. The build is budget-first and done in-house with a chop saw and angle grinder, both to avoid high outsourcing quotes and to develop hands-on manufacturing skills.

Project Story

Situation & Goal

Build a reliable first kart from scratch on a student budget, document the process, and end with something we can start, drive, and maintain.

Direction

Non-shifter 4-stroke for simplicity and safety on the first build. Live axle with a 30-series TAV for predictable launches and low maintenance.

Key Decisions — and Why

Round → Square tubing: moved to 1×1×.120″ A513 square to avoid tube bending, keep cuts repeatable, make jigging/alignment straightforward, and stay on budget.
Engine swap → Lifan 212 cc ES: considered Predator 212 but electric-start conversion adds parts, cost, and time. Lifan comes electric-start out-of-the-box, supports a clean push-to-start, and matches the typical 3/4″ keyed shaft/bore patterns we needed.

Actions

  • Modeled the frame as a square-tube weldment; generated drawings for tube cuts and basic brackets.
  • Laid out the drivetrain for live-axle use with a 30-series TAV to #420 chain.
  • Integrated a push-to-start into the Lifan harness with battery, fusing, relay, and kill-switch to ground ignition.
  • Fabricated in-house using chop saw + angle grinder; tack, check, and weld sequence planned to control distortion.

Results / Status

  • Chassis tube prep completed; welding/assembly in progress.
  • Push-to-start verified on the bench; harness mounting/loom in progress.
  • TAV alignment issue identified and corrected with spacers and a slotted bracket (details below).

At-a-Glance

Chassis — 1×1×.120″ A513 Square-Tube

Simple miter-and-weld approach to stay within budget and use accessible tools. Joints and tabs were detailed so fit-up is repeatable, and the weld sequence is planned to keep things straight. Welding and final assembly are underway.

Powertrain — Lifan 212 cc ES + 30-Series TAV

Lifan 212 cc electric-start with a custom push-to-start, 30-series TAV to #420 chain, and a live axle. Choice centers on reliability, available parts, and keeping the start/charging system straightforward.

Issue: TAV backplate limited belt alignment with available engine-mount slot travel.
Fix: Added spacers and a slotted secondary bracket to set center distance while keeping the TAV parallel.

Electrical — Push-to-Start Integration

Start path: battery → inline fuse at battery positive → master → push-button branch (fused) → starter relay → starter. Kill switch grounds ignition. Grounds terminate to a single chassis lug.

Issue: Starter latched once due to a shared ground path with the kill switch.
Fix: Dedicated ground strap to the starter case and separated returns; ferrule-crimped terminations.

Static checks used to sanity-check deflection/stress at known hot-spots before cutting tube. Drop screenshots here as they finalize.
