Project

Custom Go-Kart

From-scratch single-seat kart with a live axle, Lifan 212 cc electric-start powerplant with integrated push-to-start, 30-series torque converter to #420 chain, and a 1"×1"×.120" A513 square-tube weldment chassis. Designed for reliability, straightforward fabrication, and easy maintenance.

CADMetal FabricationFEACircuit Design & WiringMechanical Systems Integration

Overview

This project started as a clean, reliable kart we could build in a small shop without outsourcing. The why: learn to take a mechanical system end-to-end—CAD, manufacturing plan, fabrication, integration, test—while hitting a firm budget and schedule. We biased toward components with great serviceability and proven community support (Lifan 212 cc, 30-series TAV) and a chassis that’s strong but simple to cut and weld in-house.

Manufacturing plan was built around an accurate cut list and repeatable jigs. We used a chop saw and angle grinder for all tube prep, then MIG welded the 1×1×.120 A513 frame—zero machine-shop outsourcing. All layout, fixturing, and grinding steps were planned in CAD to remove guesswork at the bench.

Chassis & Layout

The frame is a square-tube weldment modeled as a SolidWorks weldment with a cut-list-driven workflow. Packaging checks verified engine/TAV clearances, seat/pedal ergonomics, chainline, steering sweep, and brake rotor/caliper access. Tabs/brackets were patterned to be laser-cut later, but the first build uses hand-cut brackets to keep costs down.

Powertrain & Drivetrain

The Lifan 212 cc was chosen for parts availability and electric-start. A 30-series TAV drives a #420 chain to the live axle. Sprocket/gear ratio selections target strong launch torque with a modest top speed for shakedown runs. Chainline and axle bearing mounts were set from datum features in CAD to keep alignment simple during welding.

Issue: TAV backplate interference with engine mount slot travel limited belt alignment.
Fix: Added offset spacers and a slotted secondary bracket to tune center distance while keeping the TAV parallel.

Electrical — Push-to-Start & Controls

We implemented a custom push-to-start integrated with the Lifan harness: battery → fuse → master → start button → starter relay. The kill switch shunts ignition to ground. Grounds are star-configured to a common chassis lug to avoid noise and hard-to-diagnose intermittents.

Issue: Starter circuit occasionally latched due to shared ground path with kill switch.
Fix: Separated returns; added dedicated ground strap from starter case to chassis; ferrule-crimped terminations.

FEA Checkpoints

Lightweight FEA was used to sanity-check peak loads at known hot-spots before cutting tube. Targets were stiffness and margin at the steering column mount, pedal mounts, and rear axle bearing tabs under braking/landing loads.

Manufacturing Plan

  • Tube prep: chop-saw miters + angle-grinder bevels; layout with angle blocks and stop-jigs.
  • Welding: tack in fixture → diagonal tacks → stitch sequence to control distortion → final grind and passivate.
  • Brackets: hand-cut for Rev A, patterned for future laser-cut set if we re-spin.
  • Assembly: seat, column, pedals, brakes, engine/TAV, chainline set, electrical last; shakedown with periodic torque checks.

Open Problems & Next Steps

  • Finalize welds on main frame and rear subframe; confirm flatness after cool-down.
  • Integrate battery box and master/kill switch panel; finalize harness protection and clamps.
  • Brake rotor/caliper bracket final fit; confirm pad sweep and clearance at full chain tension.
  • Seat/CG check and pedal ergonomics; add toe-board and heel stops.
  • Phase-1 testing: drivetrain heat, belt wear, chain tension stability; retorque plan.

Build Log (selected)

  • CAD → Cut list: finalized weldment, exported cut list; printed miter angles and tube lengths.
  • Frame tack-up: fixture on flat table; diagonal tacks to hold geometry; verified squareness.
  • TAV spacing: identified interference; added offset spacers + slotted bracket (see fix above).
  • Electrical proto: benchtop loom for start/kill; verified relay current and voltage drop.
  • Brake bracket: paper template → steel blank → hand-fit on axle with rotor; clearance confirmed.
