From-scratch single-seat kart built around a Lifan 212 cc electric-start with a custom push-to-start wired into the Lifan harness, a 30-series torque converter driving a live axle, and a 1×1×.120 A513 square-tube weldment chassis. The build follows a cut-list driven workflow and a budget-first plan fabricated in-house with a chop saw and angle grinder (quotes to outsource were too high).

Design Decisions & Why

Round → Square Tube

Originally explored a round-tube frame. We moved to 1×1×.120 A513 square to keep the build inside budget and tool access: no tube bender required, miters are repeatable, fixturing/jigging is simpler, and the whole frame can be driven by a clear cut-list.

Engine Change → Lifan 212 cc (Electric-Start)

Switched to the Lifan 212 cc specifically to support a reliable electric-start + push-to-start setup using the Lifan wiring harness and charging system. That choice makes the start circuit clean and serviceable, and it aligns with the project goal of a practical, testable kart (not just a pull-start prototype).

Chassis (Square-Tube Weldment)

The frame is modeled as a square-tube weldment. The cut list controls tube lengths and miters, and the joint scheme was set up for easy fit-up with basic tools. Welding/assembly is in progress.

Powertrain

Lifan 212 cc 4-stroke with electric-start, a 30-series torque converter, and a chain drive to the live axle.

Issue: TAV backplate limited belt alignment with the available engine-mount slot travel.
Fix: Added offset spacers and a slotted secondary bracket to set center distance and keep the TAV parallel.

Electrical — Push-to-Start

Start circuit integrated with the Lifan harness: battery → fuse → master → push-to-start → relay → starter. A kill switch grounds ignition; grounds are tied to a common chassis lug.

Issue: Starter occasionally latched due to a shared ground path with the kill switch.
Fix: Separated returns, added a dedicated ground strap to the starter case, and ferrule-crimped terminations.
