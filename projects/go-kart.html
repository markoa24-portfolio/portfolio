From-scratch single-seat kart built around a Lifan 212 cc electric-start with integrated push-to-start, a 30-series torque converter driving a live axle, and a 1×1×.120 A513 square-tube weldment chassis.

Overview

  • Engine: Lifan 212 cc with electric-start and a custom push-to-start wired into the Lifan harness.
  • Drivetrain: 30-series torque converter → chain → live axle.
  • Chassis: 1″×1″×.120″ A513 square-tube weldment.
  • Process: cut-list driven workflow and packaging/clearance checks in CAD.
  • Manufacturing: in-house with a chop saw and angle grinder (quotes to outsource were too high).
The scope intentionally avoids outsourcing. All tube prep and fit-up are planned from the cut list; welding and assembly are in progress.

Chassis & Packaging

The frame is modeled as a square-tube weldment. The CAD cut list drives tube lengths and miters. Packaging checks covered engine/TAV clearance, chain path to the live axle, and room for the electrical components and switches.

Powertrain

  • Lifan 212 cc 4-stroke with electric-start.
  • 30-series torque converter feeding a chain to the live axle.
  • Focus: simple, serviceable parts with reliable availability.
Issue: TAV backplate limited belt alignment when combined with engine-mount slot travel.
Fix: Added offset spacers and a slotted secondary bracket to set center distance and keep the TAV parallel.

Electrical — Push-to-Start

The start circuit is integrated with the Lifan harness: battery → fuse → master → push-to-start → relay → starter. A kill switch grounds ignition. Grounds are tied to a common chassis lug.

Issue: Starter occasionally latched due to a shared ground path with the kill switch.
Fix: Separated returns, added a dedicated ground strap to the starter case, and ferrule-crimped terminations.
