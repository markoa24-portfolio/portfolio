Custom Go-Kart

Build video — add MP4 or YouTube embed when ready

From-scratch single-seat kart using a Lifan 212 cc electric-start with a custom push-to-start tied into the Lifan harness, a 30-series torque converter to a live axle, and a 1×1×.120″ A513 square-tube weldment chassis. The build follows a cut-list workflow and a budget-first plan fabricated in-house with a chop saw and angle grinder (outsourcing quotes were too high).

Design Decisions & Why

Round → Square Tube

Switched from circular DOM/ERW to 1.00″ × 1.00″ × 0.120″ (11 GA) A513 square tubing.
Why: eliminates tube bending; simpler fabrication (cut + weld); easier jigging and alignment. (Notes: ordered 6 bars @ 10 ft = 60 ft total; cut-list required ≈477 in ≈39.8 ft, leaving ≈20 ft margin.)

Engine Change → Lifan 212 cc (Electric-Start)

Originally considered Predator 212. Converting it to electric-start would have required a ring-gear flywheel, starter motor, battery system, shroud mods, and possibly crank machining — adding cost, time, and risk.
Chosen: Lifan 212 cc Electric-Start because it comes with ES pre-installed, has the same shaft diameter and bolt pattern as the Predator (3/4″ keyed), delivers similar HP/torque, supports a starter switch/Arduino later, and fits the simple, budget-friendly, beginner-ready goal.

Chassis & Cut-List Workflow

Chassis modeled as a square-tube weldment; tube lengths and miters are controlled by the cut list. All tube prep and fit-up are planned for basic tools and in-house welding (no outsourcing).

Powertrain

  • Lifan 212 cc 4-stroke, electric-start.
  • 30-series torque converter feeding chain to a live axle.
  • Compatibility: same 3/4″ keyed shaft & bolt pattern as Predator; pairs with Comet TAV2.

Electrical — Push-to-Start

Custom push-to-start integrated with the Lifan harness: battery → inline fuse at battery positive → master → push-button branch (with smaller fuse) → starter relay → starter; kill switch to ground ignition. Notes call out battery cables, inline fuses, ring terminals, and proper crimping.

FEA (checkpoints)

Static checks on key areas to confirm acceptable deflection/stress before cutting tube. (Drop screenshots here as they finalize.)

Build Status

  • Direction set: non-shifter 4-stroke (first build focus on reliability/safety; notes cite shifter karts >100 mph / 0–60≈4s as out of scope).
  • Material finalized: 1×1×.120″ A513 square-tube; ordered 60 ft vs. ≈39.8 ft needed (cut-list ≈477 in) to allow margin.
  • Engine integrated: Lifan 212 cc ES (bench-tested via recoil and ES per notes).
  • Chassis tube prep underway; weld/assembly in progress per plan.
