Engine & Drivetrain

Final Choice: Lifan 212 cc Electric-Start (Honda GX200-style clone)

  • ~6.5 HP class, proven reliability, strong aftermarket ecosystem
  • Electric + recoil start for redundancy
  • Direct compatibility with Comet TAV2 / 30-series torque converter (3/4‚Ä≥ keyed shaft)

Reason for Switch: Predator 212 is solid, but electric-start conversion adds cost and complexity (ring-gear flywheel, starter, battery system, shroud mods, possible crank machining). Lifan ships with ES and a harness, enabling a clean push-to-start out of the box.

Chassis (Design & Manufacturing)

  • Design goals: package engine/CVT/axle/seat/pedals cleanly, resist vibration, and stay buildable for a first-time chassis.
  • Material choice: 1.00‚Ä≥ √ó 1.00‚Ä≥ √ó 0.120‚Ä≥ (11 GA) A513 square steel tubing.
  • Square tube = easier cutting, welding, and jigging vs. round tubing; no tube-bending required.
  • CAD work: modeled in SolidWorks with weldments, generated cut lists, confirmed clearances for rear axle and torque converter.
  • Structural validation: quick static stress checks indicated acceptable deflection under a conservative 180 lb driver load.
  • Manufacturing: fully in-house‚Äîcutting, grinding/beveling, tack-up, and MIG weld-out with a planned sequence to control distortion.

Steering & Braking

  • Reworked steering shaft placement for better ergonomics and control.
  • Added a U-bend crossbar as the steering mount; aligned with spindles for geometry accuracy.
  • Brake system mount integrated into the rear axle assembly for simplicity and strength.

Electrical Integration

  • Bench-tested the factory key-start, then designed a custom push-to-start circuit.
  • 12 V battery, relays, inline fuses, and a momentary push-button‚Äîbuilt and validated.
  • Engine now starts reliably from both key and push-to-start; kill switch grounds ignition; single-point chassis ground.
  • Lesson learned: this required safe system design and validation, not just ‚Äúwiring it up.‚Äù

üõ†Ô∏è Engineering Process ‚Äî Highlights (~3 months)

  • Kickoff: defined scope; pivoted from shifter kart to non-shifter for safety, scope, and cost.
  • Planning: created an internal timeline and drafted a preliminary BOM.
