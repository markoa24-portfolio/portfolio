Porous Airfoil Flow Study (USC Viterbi)

Varying porous-hole spacing/angle on NACA 0012; CAD models, ANSYS sims, and water-channel testing.

Summary

Summer research at USC Viterbi: studied how porous holes influence separation and lift/drag on a NACA 0012. We varied hole spacing (10%, 15%, 20% of chord) and tested at angles of attack (3°, 6°, 9°, 12°) near Re ≈ 5×10⁴.

Methods

  • CAD & fabrication: SolidWorks models of porous patterns; 3D printing, clean/finish, assembly.
  • Simulation: ANSYS 2D cross-section workflow (transient) with MATLAB/Excel helpers.
  • Experiment: Water-channel testing with laser sheet & high-speed camera; velocity contour comparisons.
