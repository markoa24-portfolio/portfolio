01 — About

Aerospace Engineering Student @ UCLA|

Hi, I’m Mark Agban — a third-year Aerospace Engineering student at UCLA who brings complex systems from concept to reality. I thrive in environments where ideas need to be engineered, fabricated, and proven — whether that’s in the lab, the shop, or under load.

I’m currently the Lead Accessibility Engineer at UCLA’s Disability Computing Program, where I lead campus-wide efforts to integrate accessibility into course design. I build standards, lead technical audits of complex PDFs, develop internal tools, and train instructors to create content that meets accessibility requirements without last-minute remediation.

Recent work: a from-scratch live-axle go-kart with electric start, a 30-series torque converter, a welded square-tube chassis, and a custom push-to-start. The past two summers, I’ve interned as a Manufacturing Engineer at Lamsco West and a Mechanical Engineer at Haas Automation. At Lamsco, I worked on process improvement, spec compliance, and hands-on validation of aerospace parts through manufacturing. At Haas, I developed access control systems, designed and fabricated hardware, and supported production and assembly operations on the shop floor.

When I’m not engineering, you’ll find me carving up slopes with UCLA’s snowboarding team, chasing adrenaline through skydives and bungee jumps, or training for the next Spartan race.
